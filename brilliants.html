About Brilliant Moves

7.1 — What counts. A Brilliant Move is a move annotated !! by Chess.com's analysis engine in a game played during an official season round. The move must have been a genuinely strong, non-obvious tactical or strategic find.
7.2 — Bonus points. Each verified brilliant move awards the player +4 season points, added on top of their regular round finishing score. There is no cap on the number of brilliants per season.
7.3 — No game result restriction. A Brilliant Move bonus is awarded regardless of the game's outcome. A brilliant move in a loss counts just as much as one in a win — a brilliant is a brilliant.
7.4 — Submission window. Players must submit their brilliant move claim to the organiser within 24 hours of the round ending. Include a link to the Chess.com game and the move number.
7.5 — Verification. The organiser reviews each submission. If the !! annotation is confirmed in the game analysis, the bonus is awarded and the move is added to this page. The organiser's decision is final.
7.6 — Recording. Verified brilliants appear on this page and the +4 points are added to the season standings workbook in the Brilliants column.
